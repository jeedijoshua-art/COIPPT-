Duties 1-3 Explained

Duty 1: Respect Constitution & National Symbols

Abide by the Constitution and respect National Flag & National Anthem. Show reverence during national ceremonies.

Duty 2: Cherish Freedom Struggle Ideals

Follow the noble ideals that inspired our freedom struggle - values like truth, non-violence, and sacrifice.

Duty 3: Uphold Sovereignty, Unity & Integrity

Protect the sovereignty, unity, and integrity of India. Stand against forces that try to divide our nation.

6/18
üë§ Speaker: Ahamed Afrideen A

Duties 4-6 Explained

Duty 4: Defend the Country

Defend the country and render national service when called upon. Support the nation during crises.

Duty 5: Promote Harmony & Brotherhood

Promote harmony and common brotherhood among all Indians, transcending religious, linguistic, and regional diversities. Reject practices derogatory to women's dignity.

Duty 6: Value & Preserve Composite Culture

Value and preserve the rich heritage of our composite culture. Protect India's diversity for future generations.

7/18
üë§ Speaker: Ahamed Afrideen A

Duties 7-9 Explained

Duty 7: Protect Natural Environment

Protect and improve the natural environment including forests, lakes, rivers, and wildlife. Have compassion for living creatures.

Duty 8: Develop Scientific Temper

Develop scientific temper, humanism, and spirit of inquiry and reform. Question superstitions and embrace rational thinking.

Duty 9: Safeguard Public Property

Safeguard public property and abjure violence. Damaging public property harms society and wastes taxpayer money.

8/18
üë§ Speaker: Ahamed Afrideen A

Duties 10-11 Explained

Duty 10: Strive for Excellence

Strive towards excellence in all spheres of individual and collective activity, so the nation constantly rises to higher levels of endeavor and achievement.

Duty 11: Provide Education (Added 2002)

Parents/guardians must provide opportunities for education to children between 6-14 years of age. Complements the fundamental right to education.

Note: This duty ensures no child is deprived of schooling and makes education a shared responsibility of parents and the state.
